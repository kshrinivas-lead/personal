# The Grounded Practice Toolkit

**Eight artifacts. One method. Everything a facilitator needs to run a Grounded Assessment — from the engagement letter to the board one-pager.**

Grounded is a transformation readiness framework: it checks whether an organisation's foundations are ready *before* capital is committed to an AI investment. The framework is open and citable. These artifacts are how it gets delivered in practice.

Every template is fully editable (Word / Excel). Replace the bracketed `[placeholders]`, keep the scoring rules intact, and version anything you change.

---

## How the artifacts fit together

| # | Artifact | Format | Stage | Who holds it |
|---|----------|--------|-------|--------------|
| 1 | Engagement Letter & Statement of Work | Word | Before Day 1 | Client + facilitator |
| 2 | Investment Statement Worksheet | Word | Day 1 | Sponsor |
| 3 | Private Intake Questionnaire | Word | Days 2–7 | Each role, alone |
| 4 | Scoring & Aggregation Workbook | Excel | Days 8–9 | **Facilitator only** |
| 5 | Facilitation Guide & Session Runsheet | Word | Day 10 | Facilitator |
| 6 | Assessment Report & Board One-Pager | Word | Day 10+ | Client leadership |
| 7 | Grounded Scan · Product | Excel | Upstream (pre-engagement) | Product owner |
| 8 | Product Edition — Question Map | Word | Any product engagement | Facilitator |

Artifacts 1–6 run the full assessment in sequence, roughly two weeks elapsed. Artifacts 7–8 serve the product context: the Scan discovers *where* AI should touch a product; the Question Map translates the assessment for a specific AI capability on a roadmap.

## 1. Engagement Letter & Statement of Work

**File:** `01-Engagement-Letter-SoW.docx`

### Purpose
Contracts the engagement — scope, timeline, deliverables, fees — and, critically, makes the method's non-negotiables *contractual* before anyone answers a single question.

### Concepts it carries
- **The two-week arc** — kickoff, private intake, aggregation, the 90-minute session, reporting.
- **The facilitator's contract as a clause** — individual answers are confidential to the facilitator; disagreement is reported only as a pattern by role category, never attributed to a named individual. Putting this in the SoW means it isn't a courtesy the client can waive mid-session.
- **What Grounded is not** — it does not evaluate the technology, the vendor, or the solution. A verdict of Grounded eliminates the most preventable causes of failure; it guarantees nothing. Setting this expectation in writing prevents the report being read as a technology endorsement.

### How to use it
Issue before Day 1. Confirm the sponsor is empowered to land the Investment Statement, and that 4–6 roles can give twenty private minutes plus the ninety-minute session.

**Practical tips**
- Do not soften the facilitator's contract clause to win the deal. It is the engagement's only defence against honest answers drying up — and the first thing a nervous middle manager will test.
- The client responsibility that gets missed: *screen access for the live anchor-question demonstration*. Chase it at signing, not on session morning.
- Price re-grounding into the original letter. Selling it later, after the 90-day expiry, feels like an upsell; selling it upfront frames it as what it is — part of the method.

## 2. Investment Statement Worksheet

**File:** `02-Investment-Statement-Worksheet.docx`

### Purpose
Produces the single sentence every subsequent question is answered against: *"We are investing — so that — can —, measured by —."* No sentence, no assessment.

### Concepts it carries
- **The one-sentence discipline** — amount, specific beneficiary, specific capability, falsifiable metric with a from → to movement.
- **Scoping failure as finding number one** — if the leadership team cannot agree the sentence in one call, stop. That disagreement, surfaced in five minutes instead of eighteen months, is already the assessment paying for itself.
- **Debt Item Zero** — "not yet costed" is an acceptable amount, but it is logged as the first entry in the debt register. An organisation that cannot bound its own spend has told you something real.

### How to use it
Sponsor completes it on Day 1, time-boxed to one call. The four quality tests on the worksheet are pass/fail — run all four before accepting the sentence.

**Practical tips**
- The most common failure is the beneficiary: "the business" or "the organisation" always fails the test. Push until a named team or role appears.
- Watch for metrics that cannot be falsified ("improve efficiency"). If the number can't prove the investment *wrong*, it isn't a metric.
- Paste the final sentence into the header of every intake form before distribution — respondents should never answer in the abstract.

## 3. Private Intake Questionnaire

**File:** `03-Private-Intake-Questionnaire.docx`

### Purpose
Collects each role's answers to the thirty evidence questions — alone, in confidence, in about twenty minutes. This is where the raw material of the whole assessment comes from.

### Concepts it carries
- **The Evidence Rule** — a Yes counts only if the respondent can name the artifact that proves it: a document, a dashboard, a named person, a date. A Yes that cannot name its artifact is a Partially. The form enforces this structurally: every Yes has a mandatory artifact field.
- **Diagnostic mode, not reflection mode** — the questions carry the expertise so the respondent doesn't have to. A non-technical leader can answer "can you pull this report right now?" by looking at what exists.
- **Private intake against the HiPPO** — answered alone, no conferring. The overlap design (every role answers the three gate blocks) is deliberate: it is what creates fault lines.
- **The freshness rule** — evidence older than twelve months records as Partially.

### How to use it
Distribute one copy per role, individually, with a return deadline. Forms come back to the facilitator only — never copied to colleagues, never discussed.

**Practical tips**
- Send the forms *individually*, not to a distribution list. A group email invites a group answer.
- Tell respondents upfront that one question will later be demonstrated live on screen. It is the cheapest calibration device in the method — hopeful Yeses drop measurably.
- If a respondent asks a colleague what they answered, that is a contract breach — address it before aggregation, not in the session.
- Twenty minutes is the promise. If a form takes an hour, the respondent is drafting a defence, not naming artifacts. Say so in the covering note.

## 4. Scoring & Aggregation Workbook

**File:** `Grounded-Scoring-Workbook-v1.0.xlsx`

### Purpose
The engine. Turns a stack of private intake forms into block scores, gate results, fault lines, a calibration check, a verdict, a debt register and an expiry date — automatically. One person, one spreadsheet, one hour.

### Concepts it carries
- **Scoring** — Yes = 2, Partially = 1, No = 0; thirty questions; sixty points.
- **The three gates** — unit economics (Block 3), data & context (Block 5), adoption & change (Block 7). A gate passes only if the block scores ≥ 4/6 *and* the proximity role recorded no "No" in it.
- **Proximity beats seniority** — on each gate, the role closest to the evidence carries double weight: Finance on economics, Technical on data, Frontline on adoption. The CFO's view of data readiness does not outweigh the person who runs the pipeline.
- **Fault lines** — every question where roles split, graded automatically: *Critical* (proximity role says No, a senior role says Yes, on a gate), *Significant* (clean Yes/No split), *Minor* (freshness or retrievability gaps). Two organisations can return the same score and face opposite prospects — the disagreement, not the score, is the signal.
- **The calibration flag** — any role answering 27+ of 30 questions Yes is flagged for two artifact spot-checks. Quality control, not accusation.
- **The verdict** — Grounded (≥ 48, all gates pass, no critical fault line) · Conditionally Grounded (any failed gate or critical split, or 30–47) · Ungrounded (two failed gates, or under 30). Gates and critical splits override the score.
- **90-day expiry** — computed from the session date. A verdict is a snapshot, not a certificate.

### How to use it
Facilitator's hands only. Set up roles and proximity slots on SETUP, transcribe every intake form into INTAKE, then read — everything downstream computes. Yellow cells are the only inputs.

**Practical tips**
- **Never use the workbook to collect answers.** Respondents in a shared file — even on separate tabs — can see each other's answers, and the private intake collapses. Forms in, transcription by the facilitator, workbook stays closed.
- Do not share the provisional verdict with anyone before the session. The verdict is revealed last for a reason: once the room knows the answer, artifacts stop appearing.
- Check the calibration flag *before* planning the session — the spot-checks open the meeting, and they need a neutral script ready.
- Sort the FAULT LINES tab by severity when preparing; critical and significant splits are the session's agenda.
- The scoring rules are versioned like a standard. If you change a threshold, say so in the report — a silent recalibration destroys comparability across assessments.

## 5. Facilitation Guide & Session Runsheet

**File:** `04-Facilitation-Guide-Runsheet.docx`

### Purpose
Scripts the ninety-minute session, minute by minute — from reading the facilitator's contract aloud to revealing the verdict last — plus the pre-session aggregation checklist and a playbook for the moments that go wrong.

### Concepts it carries
- **Open on disagreement, never on a blank canvas** — the session starts with the fault-line pattern, by role category.
- **Artifacts, not opinions** — every contested answer gets the same request: *"Can someone put the artifact on screen?"* Then silence. If it appears, resolved. If it doesn't, a confirmed gap. Nobody is ever asked who answered what.
- **The anchor question, live** — one evidence question is demonstrated on screen in real time. A Yes produced live is real; a Yes that "could be pulled together by Thursday" is a Partially, and everyone has just learned why.
- **The debt register, built in the room** — every confirmed gap becomes a priced, owned, dated debt item before the session ends.
- **The verdict comes last** — computed, not negotiated. What is negotiable is the debt register.

### How to use it
Run the Part A checklist in the days before the session; follow the runsheet on the day; keep the difficult-moments playbook printed beside you.

**Practical tips**
- Read the facilitator's contract *verbatim* at the open, even to a friendly room. The recitation is the protection.
- When a senior voice contests a No, never argue — ask for the artifact. The screen settles what the room cannot.
- Let the silence after "can someone put it on screen?" run longer than is comfortable. The silence is doing the work.
- If Conditionally Grounded, say the sentence exactly: *"Proceed, with the gaps priced in and the gate debt closed before capital is released."* Delivered any softer, it is heard as a no; any firmer, as a yes.
- Leave the session with three things fixed: the debt register complete, the re-grounding date in calendars, and the intake forms secured or destroyed per the contract.

## 6. Grounded Assessment Report & Board One-Pager

**File:** `05-Assessment-Report-Board-OnePager.docx`

### Purpose
The permanent record and the decision instrument: verdict, block scores, fault-line summary, foundation debt, expiry, re-grounding path — with a one-page version built for the board pack.

### Concepts it carries
- **Bluntness at the headline, precision underneath** — the verdict leads; the score is working material, never the headline.
- **Foundation debt belongs in the business case** — the register total is added to the investment case before it goes to the board. A case that doesn't carry its foundation debt is mispriced.
- **Senior debt** — gate-block items are closed *before* capital release, not in parallel with the build.
- **Anonymity survives into the report** — fault lines appear by role category only. Forever.
- **Expiry on the cover** — readiness decays; the report says when this one does.

### How to use it
Draft within days of the session while the register is fresh. The one-pager is the last page — detach it for the board pack; the full report stands behind it for anyone who asks.

**Practical tips**
- The "Decision requested" line on the one-pager is the most important sentence you will write. *Approve with staged capital against debt closure* is usually the honest option for a Conditionally Grounded verdict — it turns the register into the release schedule.
- Resist adding recommendations about the technology or vendor. The moment the report opines on the solution, it stops being a readiness verdict and starts being a consulting opinion.
- Put the expiry date on the cover and in the one-pager. A report without its expiry gets quoted at month eleven.

## 7. Grounded Scan · Product

**File:** `Grounded-Scan-Product-v1.0.xlsx`

### Purpose
The lightweight, solo-runnable precursor for product owners asking *"is this product ready for AI enablement — and where, if anywhere, should AI touch it?"* It runs Block 1's core question in breadth across the product's workflows and returns a shortlist of candidates, each with a rough Investment Statement.

### Concepts it carries
- **Scan ≠ Assessment** — the Scan finds where to look; only the full assessment tests whether what you found is ready to fund. No gates, no private intake, no fault lines, no verdict. Kept separate on purpose: bending the full framework's discipline to accommodate discovery would quietly destroy the discipline.
- **Six screening questions per workflow** — frequency (>50×/week across the user base), variance (outcome differs by user), documented (what users do today is written down), data exhaust (event data retrievable without an engineering ticket), load-bearing (a core job-to-be-done changes — not decorative), value line (a nameable metric it would move).
- **Auto-triage** — 9–12 SHORTLIST · 6–8 PARK · under 6 DROP.
- **The data-rights flag** — can customer data contractually be used for AI on this workflow, per tenant? A "?" on a shortlisted row becomes Debt Item Zero when the candidate graduates.
- **The rough Investment Statement** — assembled automatically from four fields; "not yet costed" is honest and acceptable.

### How to use it
One product owner, 60–90 minutes. Inventory 10–25 workflows on the SCAN GRID (one row per *workflow*, not per feature idea), answer the six questions per row, carry the top 3–5 to the SHORTLIST tab, draft the rough statements, and graduate each survivor to a full Grounded Assessment (Product Edition).

**Practical tips**
- Breadth beats depth. Fifteen honestly-screened workflows outperform three lovingly-argued ones — the Scan's value is in what it *rules out*.
- The rows that fail S3 (nothing documented) and S4 (no event data) cluster. That cluster is not a rejection of AI; it is a map of the instrumentation work the product needs first.
- A high Scan score is a reason to run the full assessment, never a substitute for it. Do not let a 12/12 row skip the gates.
- Resist filling the grid in a group workshop. The Scan is deliberately solo — its honesty comes from nobody watching.

## 8. Product Edition — Question Map

**File:** `07-Product-Edition-Question-Map.docx`

### Purpose
Translates the thirty evidence questions for assessing a specific AI capability on a product's roadmap. Same method, same gates, same scoring, same verdict — different language, so a Product Edition verdict stays comparable with a manufacturing or neutral one.

### Concepts it carries
- **Editions translate language, never method** — ten questions are rewritten; twenty carry over verbatim. The framework descends from the AI Product Canvas, and most questions never left home.
- **The adoption gate, rewritten deepest** — from internal job roles to external user segments: research sessions and beta cohorts as consultation evidence, a customer-success playbook and a working non-AI fallback path as the resistance plan, a previously shipped behaviour-changing feature (with its adoption number) as the track record.
- **Willingness to pay enters the economics gate** — packaging and pricing (included, upsell, add-on) join cost-per-inference in Q7.
- **Data rights folded into governance** — Q23 now asks whether the contractual right to use customer data, per tenant (DPAs, training and retention clauses), has been reviewed in writing. Folded rather than added, to keep the 30-question, 60-point architecture intact.
- **The product anchor question** — Q13, demonstrated live: *"Pull, right now — without an engineering ticket — the last 30 days of event data for the exact user workflow this AI will touch: timestamped, per user, per tenant, with no missing steps."*
- **The Product Edition role map** — Finance/RevOps on economics, Data/platform engineering on data, Customer Success or UX research on adoption (the product's "frontline").

### How to use it
Overlay, not replacement: run intake with the Product Edition wording, transcribe into the standard Scoring Workbook unchanged, set the proximity slots to the Product Edition roles, and substitute Q13 as the live demonstration.

**Practical tips**
- The product owner sponsors the assessment — and must not be its only respondent. A solo run has no fault lines, and the fault lines are the point.
- Q21 (a shipped behaviour-changing feature with a named adoption number) is where product teams most often discover they have never actually measured feature adoption. That discovery is itself a finding.
- Any "?" on data rights is senior debt. It blocks capital release exactly like a gate gap — a capability the product may not legally power is not a foundation issue you fix in phase two.

## Three rules that run through everything

1. **Evidence over opinion.** A Yes without a nameable artifact is a Partially — on every form, in every edition, with no exceptions for seniority.
2. **The pattern, never the person.** Disagreement is the most valuable output of the method, and it survives only as long as no answer is ever traced to a name.
3. **Grounding expires.** Ninety days. Readiness decays, people move, models deprecate. The verdict is a snapshot; the re-grounding is the habit.
